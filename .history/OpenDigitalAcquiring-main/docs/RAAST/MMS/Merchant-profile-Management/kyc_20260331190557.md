# Merchant KYC (Know Your Customer)

Merchant KYC is the process of verifying the identity of merchants to prevent fraud, ensure compliance with financial regulations, and mitigate risks. This process is crucial for establishing trust and ensuring that the merchant meets the required standards set by the Authorized Financial Institution or Regulatory Authority.

## **KYC Process for Merchant Onboarding**

The KYC process for merchant onboarding involves several steps, including identity verification, document submission, and risk assessment. These steps are designed to validate the legitimacy of the merchant and ensure that their business complies with regulatory requirements.

### **Key KYC Verification Steps**

#### **1. Personal Identification**
- **Action**: The merchant is required to provide personal identification details to verify their identity.
- **Details**:
  - **Full Name**: The legal name of the business owner or authorized representative.
  - **Date of Birth**: The birthdate of the business owner or representative.
  - **Government-Issued ID Number**: A unique identifier issued by a government authority (e.g., passport number, national ID number).

#### **2. Business Identification**
- **Action**: The merchant’s business details are verified.
- **Details**:
  - **Business Registration Number**: The official business registration number provided by the relevant government authority.
  - **DBA (Doing Business As) Name**: The trade name under which the business operates.
  - **Tax Identification Number (TIN)**: A unique identifier used for tax purposes.

#### **3. Address Verification**
- **Action**: The merchant must provide proof of their business address.
- **Details**:
  - **Business Address**: The physical location of the business.
  - **Supporting Document**: A utility bill, lease agreement, or other official documents that verify the business address.

#### **4. Financial Information**
- **Action**: The merchant must provide their financial details to ensure their bank accounts are valid and linked to their business operations.
- **Details**:
  - **Bank Account Information**: The merchant’s **IBAN**, **Account Title**, and **Branch Code**.
  - **Proof of Account Ownership**: A recent bank statement or document verifying the account ownership.

#### **5. Risk Assessment**
- **Action**: The merchant’s risk profile is assessed to determine the level of scrutiny required.
- **Details**:
  - **Transaction History**: A review of the merchant’s historical transaction data (if available).
  - **Source of Funds**: Information regarding the origin of funds, ensuring no illicit activities are involved.
  - **Business Nature**: The type of business and its compliance with industry standards.

### **Required Documents for KYC**
The following documents are typically required for the KYC process:
- **Government-issued ID** (Passport, National ID, Driver's License)
- **Business Registration Certificate** or **Articles of Incorporation**
- **Tax Identification Number (TIN)**
- **Proof of Business Address** (Utility Bill, Lease Agreement)
- **Bank Account Proof** (Recent Bank Statement)
- **Selfie or Photo with ID** (in some cases, to validate identity)


## **KYC Compliance and Regulatory Requirements**

### **Global KYC Standards**
The KYC process must comply with global financial regulations, including the following:

- **Anti-Money Laundering (AML) Regulations**: To prevent the illegal movement of funds, businesses must adhere to AML standards.
- **Know Your Business (KYB)**: Ensures that the business’s identity is verified, and their operational legitimacy is established.
- **Sanctions and Watchlists**: Merchants must be checked against international sanctions and watchlists to ensure compliance.

### **Regulatory Bodies**
KYC requirements vary depending on the country or region. However, the following global and regional regulatory bodies provide the framework for KYC practices:

- **Financial Action Task Force (FATF)**: Provides global standards for AML and KYC.
- **Local Financial Regulators**: Various countries have their own regulatory bodies (e.g., **FCA** in the UK, **SEC** in the US) that enforce KYC and AML regulations.


## **Configurable KYC Process**

Our system allows for flexible configuration of KYC processes depending on the **merchant type** and **regulatory environment**:

- **KYC Levels**: The system can enforce **basic**, **standard**, or **enhanced KYC** based on the risk profile of the merchant.
- **KYC Validation Methods**: Depending on the jurisdiction, the system may integrate with third-party KYC services to validate merchant documents, biometric data, or other verification methods.
- **Document Review Workflow**: The review and approval process for merchant documents can be configured to include a single-stage or multi-stage approval process, depending on the level of compliance required.
