# Merchant Types

In our system, Merchant Types are highly configurable to accommodate a wide variety of business models. This flexibility allows the system to support different categories of merchants, each with its own set of requirements, access levels, and transaction capabilities. Merchant types define how a merchant interacts with the system, what features they can access, and the level of security required for their operations.

## Merchant Types in Our System

Our system supports the following **configurable Merchant Types**, each designed to meet the unique needs of different business models. The system allows administrators to add, modify, or remove merchant types according to business requirements.

### Configurable Merchant Types:

| **Merchant Type**       | **Description**                                         | **KYC Requirements**                             | **Transaction Limits**                            |
|-------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------|--------------------------------------------------|--------------------------------------------------|
| **Individual**           | A single business owner or sole proprietor.             | Basic KYC with personal identification.          | Lower transaction limits, subject to approval.   |
| **Light KYC**            | Informal or semi-formal businesses with light checks.   | Simplified KYC with geo-tagging.                 | Moderate transaction limits.                    |
| **Company**              | Formal, registered business entities.                  | Full KYC with business registration and documents. | Higher transaction limits, complex limits.       |
| **Government-verified**  | Businesses that require government-level verification. | Comprehensive government verification (e.g., NIDA or equivalent). | High transaction limits with extra security checks. |
| **Corporate**            | Large-scale corporations with multiple branches.       | Advanced KYC with business and tax registration. | High transaction volumes with special access and limits. |

---

### Key Features of Configurable Merchant Types:
1. **Flexibility**: Merchant types can be configured dynamically based on the needs of the business.
2. **Scalability**: The system supports a wide range of merchant types, from small sole proprietors to large multinational corporations.
3. **Role-Based Access**: Different merchant types may have varying levels of access to features based on their business type and KYC compliance.
4. **Transaction Processing**: Each merchant type can have customized transaction limits, processing rules, and fees based on their category and compliance level.

### How It Works:
- **Administrators**: Can add or modify merchant types from the **MMP UI** (Merchant Management Portal) under the **Configuration** settings.
- **System Behavior**: When a merchant is onboarded, the **Merchant Type** is assigned based on the provided business model, documents, and KYC data.

---

### Conclusion:
Our system’s configurable merchant types ensure that we can cater to a wide variety of business models, while maintaining security, compliance, and efficient transaction processing. By customizing merchant types, businesses can be onboarded smoothly, with the appropriate access levels and transaction capabilities in place.
