# Merchant Details

Merchant Details refer to the key information provided by the merchant during the onboarding process. This includes personal, business, and banking details, as well as additional information relevant to the merchant’s operations. These details are used to verify the merchant’s identity, business type, and financial information, ensuring compliance with regulations and proper account setup.

### Required Merchant Details

#### **1. Merchant Identification**
- **Merchant Name**: The official name of the business.
- **Doing Business As (DBA) Name**: The trade name under which the merchant operates.
- **Merchant ID (MID)**: A unique identifier assigned to the merchant by the **Authorized Financial Institution**.
- **Nature of Business**: The category or industry the merchant operates in (e.g., retail, services, e-commerce).
  
#### **2. Contact Information**
- **Business Address**: The physical address of the business location.
- **Email Address**: Contact email for merchant communication.
- **Phone Number**: A contact phone number for the business.

#### **3. Banking Details**
- **IBAN (International Bank Account Number)**: The merchant’s bank account number used for payment processing.
- **Branch Code**: The bank's specific branch code.
- **Account Title**: The name under which the account is held (typically the same as the business name).
- **Payee Name**: The individual or organization receiving payments.

#### **4. Financial Information**
- **Expected Monthly Revenue**: The estimated revenue the merchant expects to generate each month.
- **Settlement Type**: Indicates whether funds will be settled instantly or after a period of time (e.g., Instant/Non-Instant).
- **MDR (Merchant Discount Rate)**: The fees charged by the payment processor for processing payments, typically expressed as a percentage of the transaction amount.

#### **5. Additional Details**
- **Logo**: The merchant’s logo for branding and identification purposes.
- **Tax Identification Number (TIN)**: A unique identifier for tax purposes (if applicable).
  
Once the merchant provides these details, the information is validated and entered into the system for processing. These details are crucial for establishing the merchant’s account with the **Authorized Financial Institution** and enabling payment processing.


## **Purpose of Collecting Merchant Details**

The collection of **Merchant Details** serves multiple purposes:

1. **Verification and Compliance**: Ensures that the merchant meets all regulatory requirements (e.g., KYC and AML) as set by the **Authorized Financial Institution**.
2. **Account Setup**: Establishes the merchant’s account and allows for the correct routing of payments.
3. **Risk Assessment**: Helps assess the merchant’s transaction volume, potential risks, and fees.
4. **Audit and Reporting**: Provides a record for audits and compliance reviews, ensuring transparency and accountability.


## **Configurable Merchant Details**

Our system allows for flexible configuration of merchant details based on the **merchant type** (e.g., Individual, Company, Government-Verified). This flexibility enables tailoring the onboarding process to meet the needs of various business types and regulatory environments.

### **Customizable Information Fields:**
- **Business Nature** and **Expected Revenue** fields can be adapted based on the type of business and transaction volume.
- **MDR Types** can vary, allowing the system to accommodate different fee structures depending on the merchant’s business model and payment processing needs.
- **Settlement Type** (Instant/Non-Instant) can be configured depending on the type of merchant and their operational requirements.
