# Child Merchant

A Child Merchant is a business entity that operates under the oversight of a Parent Merchant. Child merchants are typically subsidiaries, regional branches, or specific business units within the merchant hierarchy. They have certain operational autonomy but are bound by the rules, policies, and compliance requirements set by the Parent Merchant. The Child Merchant is responsible for day-to-day operations, transactions, and customer interactions within their defined scope.

## **Key Responsibilities of a Child Merchant**

### **1. Day-to-Day Operations**
- The **Child Merchant** handles all operational tasks related to its specific business unit, including sales, customer service, inventory management, and order fulfillment.
- **Action**: The Child Merchant ensures that its operations are aligned with the Parent Merchant’s business standards and regulatory compliance requirements.

### **2. Transaction Processing**
- Child Merchants process transactions within their jurisdiction, whether **instantly** or through other **settlement types** as defined by the Parent Merchant.
- **Action**: The Child Merchant ensures all transactions are processed smoothly and meet financial compliance requirements.

### **3. Compliance with Parent Merchant Policies**
- The **Child Merchant** is required to follow the policies set by the **Parent Merchant** to ensure consistency in operations, branding, and financial activities.
- **Action**: The Child Merchant adheres to regulatory and internal business compliance standards (such as **KYC**, **AML**, and security protocols).

### **4. Data Entry and Reporting**
- Child Merchants are responsible for entering their operational data (sales, transaction volumes, customer interactions) and ensuring that reporting is accurate.
- **Action**: The Child Merchant submits reports, either manually or automatically, to the Parent Merchant for review and consolidation.


## **Key Features of Child Merchant Role**

| **Feature**                 | **Description**                                                   | **Permission Level**           |
|-----------------------------|-------------------------------------------------------------------|--------------------------------|
| **Operational Autonomy**    | Child merchants can manage day-to-day operations, including customer service, sales, and order processing. | Limited to their region or department. |
| **Transaction Processing**  | Handles the processing of transactions and follows settlement procedures defined by the Parent Merchant. | Full access to transaction processing within their scope. |
| **Compliance Adherence**    | Must comply with the policies and regulatory standards set by the Parent Merchant. | Follows Parent Merchant compliance protocols. |
| **Data Reporting**          | Submits operational data to the Parent Merchant for consolidation and analysis. | Limited to submitting data relevant to their operations. |


## **System Support for Child Merchant**

### **Role-Based Access Control (RBAC)**

The system allows the **Parent Merchant** to configure the **access levels** for **Child Merchants**. These levels determine what actions the Child Merchant can perform and which areas of the system they can access.

| **Role**          | **Permissions**                                                    | **Description**                                                     |
|-------------------|--------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|
| **Manager**       | Access to transaction processing, order management, and sales data. | Responsible for overseeing operations and ensuring smooth workflows. |
| **Viewer**        | Read-only access to reports and customer data.                    | Can view reports but cannot modify any system data.                |
| **Operator**      | Access to transaction processing and customer interactions.       | Manages day-to-day transactions and customer service.              |


### **Transaction Management**

The **Child Merchant** handles all transactions within their scope, including payment processing, refunds, and settlements. They follow the **Parent Merchant's** predefined rules and configurations regarding:

1. **Settlement Type**: The type of settlement (e.g., **instant** or **non-instant**) is determined by the **Parent Merchant**, and the Child Merchant adheres to this.
2. **Transaction Limits**: The Child Merchant may have defined transaction limits, either by volume or amount, which can be modified based on the **Parent Merchant's** configuration.

| **Transaction Type** | **Description**                                                  | **Transaction Limits**        |
|----------------------|------------------------------------------------------------------|--------------------------------|
| **Instant Settlement**| Transactions processed and settled immediately.                  | Lower limits for small merchants, as defined by Parent Merchant. |
| **Non-Instant Settlement** | Transactions that settle after a predefined period (e.g., 24 hours). | Higher limits allowed for established merchants. |


## **Merchant Hierarchy and Reporting**

### **Reporting for Child Merchants**
Child Merchants are responsible for generating their own reports and submitting them to the **Parent Merchant** for analysis. These reports help track sales, transactions, and operational performance.

- **Sales Reports**: Child Merchants generate reports on daily/weekly/monthly sales, including transaction amounts and volumes.
- **Transaction Reports**: Tracks all transactions processed, highlighting any failed or rejected transactions for further review.
- **Compliance Reports**: Ensures that all operations comply with internal policies and regulatory standards.

| **Report Type**   | **Description**                                             | **Access Level**       |
|-------------------|-------------------------------------------------------------|------------------------|
| **Sales Report**  | Provides detailed sales data for the Child Merchant.        | Child Merchant         |
| **Transaction Report** | Provides transaction details and status updates.            | Child Merchant         |
| **Compliance Report** | Tracks compliance activities, such as KYC and AML checks.   | Child Merchant         |


## **Conclusion**

The **Child Merchant** is a crucial part of the **merchant hierarchy**, handling day-to-day operations, transaction processing, and reporting while adhering to the guidelines and policies set by the **Parent Merchant**. With configurable roles, transaction limits, and flexible reporting, the **Child Merchant** can efficiently manage their operations while ensuring compliance with global regulatory standards.
